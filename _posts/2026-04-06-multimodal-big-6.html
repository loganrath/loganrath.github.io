Suggested tools by modality

These tools can help you build or adapt activities in each modality. Pick tools that fit your students, device access, and privacy requirements.

Indicators show whether a tool is free or free with paid upgrades, and whether it is usually best for teacher use or can be student-safe with teacher setup.

Linguistic/Alphabetic

  • CRISP BuilderFreeTeacher usePlan and refine prompts before pasting them into an AI tool.
  • ClaudeFree + paidTeacher useDraft passages, sentence frames, word lists, and teacher directions.
  • GeminiFree + paidTeacher useGenerate decodable text, comprehension questions, and vocabulary supports.

Visual

  • CanvaFree + paidTeacher useCreate cards, posters, charts, and picture supports for literacy lessons.
  • Adobe ExpressFree + paidTeacher useBuild quick graphics, story sequence images, and classroom visuals.
  • Microsoft DesignerFree + paidTeacher useGenerate simple image-based materials from short prompts.

Audio

  • ElevenLabsFree + paidTeacher useCreate model read-alouds, pronunciation examples, or short listening scripts.
  • NotebookLMFreeTeacher useTurn notes into spoken summaries or audio-style explanations.

  • LoomFree + paidTeacher useCreate short teacher demonstrations that show motions, tracking, or acting.
  • SeesawFree + paidStudent-safeCapture student responses that combine movement, voice, and reflection.

Spatial

  • PadletFree + paidStudent-safeOrganize ideas on boards, maps, and columns for sorting and sequencing.
  • MiroFree + paidTeacher useBuild digital organizers, word webs, and movement pathways on a shared canvas.

Before classroom use, review each tool for age requirements, accessibility features, district privacy policies, and whether your local setup is appropriate for students.

Implementation tips

  • Start with one or two combinations each week instead of trying to use the full chart at once.
  • Review every AI output before using it so the wording, examples, and length fit your students.
  • Rotate across the five modalities so students practice literacy in more than one way.
  • Keep a short list of prompts that worked well so you can revise and reuse them later.

Based on NYS Science of Reading Briefs and a multimodal literacies framework. Created for K-1 educators.
